Analytical Briefing Note | Policy audience | Source: Hub v4 | April 2026

Purpose. This note analyses the Russian occupation's ghost pensioner scheme in Mariupol: systematic overclaiming of pension payments for non-present individuals, the financial extraction mechanism, and the concurrent weaponisation of pensioners' citizenship precarity as a tool of social control. The scheme has a dual accountability dimension — it constitutes fraud against the Russian federal budget and theft of civilian welfare resources under occupation, both potentially actionable accountability vectors.

4. The Fraud Mechanism

The scheme exploits three structural features of the occupation administration:

  • DNR bank opacity. Pension payments flow through 'DNR banks' which sit outside standard Russian federal treasury audit procedures — ensuring the discrepancy is invisible to Moscow's normal oversight mechanisms.
  • Inflated population figures. The broader pattern of population figure manipulation (documented in the Hub's 2022–2024 analytical entry) shows that inflated population data serves multiple purposes: justifying federal budget flows, enabling embezzlement at scale, and creating a statistical narrative of a functioning city.
  • No return mechanism for the defrauded. The ~134,600 ghost pensioners are largely displaced Mariupol residents now in Ukrainian-controlled territory or abroad. They cannot reclaim their pension entitlements, and the Russian state continues to claim the payments in their name.

5. The Coercion Dimension

The October 2025 deportation panic reveals a second function of the pension system: as a tool of coercion. The citizenship-stripping mechanism — which targets pensioners who have maintained contact with the Ukrainian pension system — creates a structural threat that the occupation administration activates selectively to produce social destabilisation. The mechanism does not need to be applied to be effective: the threat of losing Russian citizenship (and with it all social rights, including access to pensions, healthcare, and housing) is sufficient to coerce compliance and suppress resistance.

The deliberate information panic (activated via local bloggers, not official channels) demonstrates that this is a managed coercion tool, not an automatic legal process — used when the administration wants to generate social pressure without formal accountability.

6. Accountability Vectors

The ghost pensioner scheme is analytically notable for generating two independent accountability pathways:

  • Fraud against the Russian federal budget. The extraction of ~1.68 billion rubles/month through DNR bank opacity for non-present individuals constitutes systematic financial fraud against the Russian state — potentially actionable under Russian criminal law, and exploitable as an internal political lever.
  • Violation of international humanitarian law. The diversion of resources designated for civilian welfare in occupied territory, combined with the weaponisation of pensioners' citizenship precarity, is consistent with patterns of collective punishment and may constitute violations of Geneva Convention obligations regarding civilian welfare under occupation.
  • The March 2026 apartment allocation (140 units to the 80+ cohort) is analytically significant: it confirms the presence of this population and the occupation administration's awareness of it — making implausible any future denial that elderly Ukrainian residents are a known, present group.
